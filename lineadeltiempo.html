Paraguay Time Line

1537: Aug 15, Juan de Salazar, Spanish adventurer, founded Asuncion, the capital of Paraguay

The first Europeans to arrive in Paraguay were Spanish, this was in 1516. The first colonies were founded by Domingo Martínez de Irala in 1536–56. The first Spanish colonists, unsuccessful in their search for gold, mixed peacefully among the Guarani. The Spanish adventurer Juan de Salazar de Espinosa founded the colony of Asunción on 15 August 1537. The city became the center of the Spanish colonial region of Paraguay.

1540: The Guarani were one of the first indigenous people to be approached after the arrival of Europeans in South America about 500 years ago.

The Guarani lived between the Paraguay and Paraná rivers long before Europeans arrived in the 1520s. The Guarani were one of the first indigenous people to be approached after the arrival of Europeans in South America about 500 years ago. Spanish explorers mixed peacefully with the Guarani. It is a very spiritual group. Most communities have a house of prayer and a spiritual leader, whose authority is based more on status than actual power.

1811: Aug 14, Paraguay declared independence from Spain.

Between May 14 and 15, 1811, a military strike was held against the then governor of Spain, Bernado de Velazco, which led to a government that initiated independence from the Spanish empire. In 1810 Buenos Aires abandoned the Spanish rule and tried to bring the Spanish royalty under its own rule. Paraguayans refused to accept this rule from Buenos Aires and fought off. Then, in 1811, Paraguay declared its independence from Spain.

1928: Dec 5, full-scale war with Bolivia. Both countries moved troops into the Chaco Boreal

It was a war between Bolivia and Paraguay. It happened in 1928 on the Chaco Boreal, a jungle region of about 259,000 km. The conflict started because of the War of the Pacific, in which Chile defeated Bolivia. Later, Bolivia tried to break out of its situation through the Río de La Plata approach to the Atlantic coast. That route lay the Gran Chaco, which the Bolivians thought had large oil reserves. Paraguay formally announced war on May 10, 1933. Estigarribia launched a series of attacks. After Bolivian counterattacks alarmed Paraguayan forces, a truce was arranged on June 12, 1935. About 100,000 men lost their lives in the war. A peace treaty was organized by the Chaco Peace Conference. In April 2009, Evo Morales and Fernando Lugo signed an accord determining the nation's border dispute over the Chaco region that had resulted in the war. The presidents agreed that the war had been brought on by foreign interests.

2022: Death of The Paraguayan drugs prosecutor: Marcelo Pecci.

The Paraguay drugs prosecutor Marcelo Pecci was killed on his honeymoon on a Colombian beach. He married Claudia Aguilera, a journalist, they got married on the 30 of April and were spending their honeymoon at a hotel on the Barú peninsula on Colombia’s coast. The two attackers came to the hotel’s private beach by jetski. Before the attack, Claudia had announced her pregnancy on social media. The killing caused alarmed all of Paraguay’s population.
