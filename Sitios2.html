Top 5 attractions in Paraguay

The Largest Hydroelectric Complex In The World

The Itaipu Dam is recognized as one of the greatest works of modern engineering, the largest Dam in production in the world, and for a long time, was also the largest in size. This enormous structure is one of the largest in the world.It even owns a carefully designed ecosystem for native birds and fish. It is significant to Paraguay because it generates around 75% of Paraguay’s electricity. A monumental construction which today is one of the main attractions located in Paragauay, with an organized tour service that includes several options of tours in the areas of engineering, technology, nature and history of the region.

The Ruins of a Jesuit Mission

Trinidad is a religious building, made in Greek, Italian and Roman style, made of red-sandstone, the Spanish drove the Jesuits (people who lived there away). The Jesuits founded the church and lived there until the spanish kicked them out. Guaraní people lived in the place after the expulsion of the Jesuits, until the collapse of the church dome was taken as an instruction by God to abandon the site. This monument is important to the country because of the cultural values it represent.Today, tourists can go to take pictures and explore the incredible history this place holds.

The Old Building

The Lopez Palace was the first emblematic building of Asuncion. Also known as the Government Palace, Presidential Palace, is the seat of the government of the Republic of Paraguay, since it is the official office of the President of the Republic.Today, you can join a tour to see the interior of the palace, which has a lot of halls and a few galleries of native artifacts.

The best National Park

Ybycui National Park is a fusion of mountain ranges with impressive waterfalls, located about only two and half hours from Asuncion. It is home to the Guaran and Escondido waterfalls, which are about 10 to 12 meters high. This park makes the perfect escape after a few days in Asuncion. This park is crucial not only to locals but to foreigners because you can find all the wuildlife you can imagine. Tourists can hike and observe the wildlife this place holds.

Spain in Paraguay

Areguá is one of the best towns in Paraguay. It is a craft town which specializes in ceramics, and is the nearest to Asunción. It is a lovely town with an elegant church at the top of a hill and full with colonial-type houses. There are streets lined with craft shops selling ceramics. The town is important to the locals because it is home to multiple art galleries and is considered an “Art” district. Tourists can visit the town to buy ceramics and admire the art galleries.
