Top 5 attractions in Paraguay

The Hidden Treasure of Paraguay

The Monday Falls are a large waterfall that is part of the Municipal Park Monday, which is located in the Presidente Franco District in Paraguay. It is absolutely one of the most impressive landmarks you'll ever come across in South America. The waterfalls are divided into three waterfalls, the largest is 40 meters high. The falls are the hidden treasure of paraguay, locals love this place because it’s not crowded at all. Today, tourists can take several tours to observe the beauty of these falls.

The Force of Paraguay

The Cerro Lagoon is a body of water that only appears every three or four summers. It became very popular in 2020 because of a huge scandal that affirmed that the lagoon had dyed red due to pollution. Many activists became unhappy and decided to do something about it. Around 2021 the lagoon was finally back to normal and really fast became one of the biggest tourist attraction of Paraguay.This was a clear example of when Paraguayans came together the goal is achieved, therefore the lagoon will be in everyone’s heart. Now, tourists pose for pics on the dock, or hire canoes to get up close to the plants.

The Largest City

Asunción, also known as the “Mother of Cities,” is one of the oldest cities in South America and capital of Paraguay. Asunción was founded on August 15, 1537, by Spanish explorers Juan de Salazar and Gonzalo de Mendoza. It’s not the kind of city everyone has on their bucket list. But there is a lot to discover. From nature to water to culture, it is a colorful city. This city holds a lot of history since it was the first colony created in what’s now Paraguay’s territory. Therefore, this city has a place in every local’s heart. There are many attractions for tourists to visit including the National Pantheon of Heroes to visit the Asunción Bay.

The building with the Most History in Paraguay

Asunción's most recognizable building, the impressive Panteón de los Héroes. Panteón de los Héroes is a great landmark in the capital of Paraguay, Asuncion. It was created in honor of the heroes of Paraguay who had fought in past wars. It also serves as a reminder to all that war is something that should be truly feared because of how many men had died in the War. This monument has a central role in life for some citizens because it is customary that after a historic event, people who live in Asuncion go to the streets with flags to celebrate. Today, people pay homage to the lost and forgotten heroes of the two great wars since gaining independence, and tourists can explore the unforgettable history this place offers.
